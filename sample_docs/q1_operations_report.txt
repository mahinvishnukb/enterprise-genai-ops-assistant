Enterprise Operations Inc. — Q1 2026 Operations Report

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Q1 2026 was a period of moderate growth with ongoing supply chain challenges. Shipment volume grew 12% quarter-over-quarter, driven by increased demand on the Toronto–Vancouver and Calgary–Montreal corridors. On-time delivery rate held at 86%, slightly below the 90% corporate target. Revenue increased 9% QoQ, slightly below volume growth due to carrier surcharges on delayed routes.

SHIPMENT PERFORMANCE
Total shipments in Q1: 1,847 (vs. 1,649 in Q4 2025)
On-time deliveries: 1,588 (86%)
Delayed deliveries: 203 (11%)
Cancelled shipments: 56 (3%)
Average delay duration: 3.8 days

TOP PERFORMING ROUTES (by on-time rate):
- New York → Montreal: 94% on-time
- Toronto → Calgary: 92% on-time
- Seattle → Toronto: 91% on-time

UNDERPERFORMING ROUTES (by delay rate):
- Chicago → Vancouver: 31% delay rate (worst performing)
- Seattle → Montreal: 24% delay rate
- Calgary → New York: 19% delay rate

KEY RISK: PORT CONGESTION IN VANCOUVER
Port congestion in Vancouver added an average of 2.3 days of delay to westbound shipments in February and March. Root cause: labour disputes and increased container volume from Asian imports. Mitigation in progress: rerouting a portion of westbound volume through Seattle port. Estimated resolution: Q2 2026.

CARRIER PERFORMANCE
Primary carrier (FastFreight Inc.) maintained 88% SLA compliance. Secondary carrier (NorthRoute Logistics) underperformed at 79% SLA compliance — contract review scheduled for April. A third carrier (PacificLink) was onboarded in February for Vancouver routes with early results showing 85% compliance.

CANCELLATION ANALYSIS
Cancellation rate remained low at 3% of total shipments, consistent with Q4. Primary cancellation causes: customer-requested holds (42%), carrier capacity issues (35%), customs delays (23%). No material change in overall carrier performance was observed.

FINANCIAL HIGHLIGHTS
Gross revenue: $4.2M (up 9% QoQ)
Carrier costs: $2.8M (up 13% QoQ due to surcharges)
Gross margin: 33% (down from 36% in Q4)
Delay-related penalties paid to customers: $84,000

Q2 2026 RECOMMENDED ACTION ITEMS
1. Renegotiate SLA terms with the Vancouver port operator before April 30.
2. Pilot a secondary carrier on the Calgary–Montreal route to reduce single-carrier dependency.
3. Implement real-time delay alerting for shipments exceeding 2-day threshold.
4. Review and renegotiate NorthRoute Logistics contract — consider termination if Q2 SLA compliance does not improve to 85%.
5. Expand Seattle rerouting capacity to absorb additional Vancouver overflow volume.
6. Target on-time delivery rate of 90% by end of Q2 through route optimization.
