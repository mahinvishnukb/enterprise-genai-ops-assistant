Enterprise Operations Inc. — HR Leave Policy (Effective January 2026)

ANNUAL LEAVE
All full-time employees accrue 20 days of paid annual leave per calendar year, credited at the start of each quarter (5 days per quarter). Part-time employees accrue leave on a pro-rata basis. Unused leave up to 5 days may be carried over into the next calendar year; any remaining balance beyond that is forfeited on December 31st. Employees may not take annual leave during their first 90 days of employment.

SICK LEAVE
Sick leave is entirely separate from annual leave. Employees receive 10 paid sick days per year with no carryover. A medical certificate is required for sick leave longer than 3 consecutive days. Employees who exhaust their sick leave entitlement may apply for unpaid medical leave of up to 30 days with manager and HR approval.

PARENTAL LEAVE
Primary caregivers receive 16 weeks of paid parental leave. Secondary caregivers receive 4 weeks of paid parental leave. Leave must commence within 12 months of the birth or adoption of a child.

BEREAVEMENT LEAVE
Employees are entitled to 5 paid bereavement days for the loss of an immediate family member (spouse, child, parent, sibling). 2 paid days are provided for extended family members.

EMERGENCY AND UNPLANNED LEAVE
Emergency leave should be reported to your manager as soon as possible. Formal documentation must be submitted to HR within 48 hours of return. Emergency leave of up to 3 days is paid; beyond that it is deducted from annual leave balance.

LEAVE REQUEST PROCEDURE
All planned leave requests must be submitted through the HR portal at least 5 business days in advance. Requests are subject to manager approval based on team capacity. During peak periods (Q4 October–December), leave requests require 10 business days notice.

ONBOARDING CHECKLIST FOR NEW EMPLOYEES
1. Complete HR intake forms within the first 3 business days.
2. Set up payroll and benefits enrollment within the first 2 weeks.
3. Complete mandatory compliance and security training within 30 days.
4. Schedule 1-on-1 with your manager to set 90-day performance goals.
5. Register for the company health insurance plan within 14 days.

REMOTE WORK POLICY
Employees may work remotely up to 3 days per week with manager approval. Full remote work arrangements require VP-level approval and are reviewed quarterly. Remote employees must be available during core hours of 10am–3pm in their local timezone.

PERFORMANCE REVIEW CYCLE
Performance reviews are conducted bi-annually: mid-year in June and year-end in December. Compensation adjustments are tied to year-end reviews. Employees receive at least 2 weeks notice before their scheduled review.
